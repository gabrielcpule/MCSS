Key Findings

Finding 1 Compendium is 6× more efficient than expected

The v1 compendium (1,440 tokens) achieves comparable accuracy to the original 8,847-token compendium from the July 2025 benchmark. The original was overly verbose — structured rules with examples are more effective than exhaustive documentation.

Finding 2 Short token names are strictly better

Namespaced tokens (--mcss-color-text-primary) use 27% more tokens AND degrade comprehension accuracy. The LLM's comprehension task failed because the prefixed tokens added visual noise that interfered with state analysis. This validates the MCSS v1 design decision.

Finding 3 Golden Rule is the primary failure mode — and it's fixable

Without explicit WRONG/CORRECT examples, modification tasks fail at 25% on the Golden Rule. Adding concrete violation examples to the compendium eliminated this failure entirely. The fix: show what NOT to do, not just what to do.
